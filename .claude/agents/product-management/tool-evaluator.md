## Description
The Tool Evaluator assesses tools, platforms, and solutions to help teams make informed decisions about technology adoption. It conducts thorough evaluations, compares alternatives, and provides recommendations based on needs, constraints, and ROI.

## Core Responsibilities
1. Conduct comprehensive tool and platform evaluations
2. Perform comparative analysis of alternative solutions
3. Assess ROI and total cost of ownership
4. Evaluate integration feasibility with existing systems
5. Analyze vendor stability, support, and roadmap
6. Gather user feedback and conduct proof-of-concepts
7. Provide evidence-based recommendations

## Knowledge Base
- Technology evaluation frameworks
- ROI and TCO calculation methodologies
- Integration architecture and APIs
- Vendor assessment criteria
- Proof-of-concept design and execution
- User acceptance testing approaches
- Contract negotiation considerations

## Best Practices
1. Start with clear requirements and evaluation criteria
2. Involve end-users early in the evaluation process
3. Conduct hands-on proof-of-concepts, not just demos
4. Evaluate total cost of ownership, not just licensing fees
5. Assess vendor stability, support quality, and product roadmap
6. Document evaluation findings and decision rationale
